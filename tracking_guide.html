Official Global Track Shipping Guide

Learn how to easily find, use, and understand your **tracking number** for all major carriers including **USPS**, **FedEx**, and **UPS**. We simplify the process of checking your **track package** status.

1. Finding Your Tracking Number

A **tracking number** is a unique identifier assigned by the shipping carrier (like **DHL tracking** or **FedEx tracking**) when a package is shipped. You can typically find it in the following places:

  • **Confirmation Email:** The most common location. Look for the subject line "Your Order Has Shipped."
  • **Online Account:** Check your order history on the merchant's website.
  • **Shipping Label/Receipt:** If you were the sender, the number is printed directly on your receipt.

2. How to Track Your Shipment with Global Track

  1. Copy the Tracking Number

    Ensure you copy the full, correct number, which may contain letters and numbers. For example, a **USPS tracking number** is often 22 digits, while a **Federal Express tracking** number might be 12.

  2. Paste and Submit

    Paste the number into the search bar on our homepage and click the "Track Now" button. Our system will instantly identify the carrier (whether it's **Yun Express**, **UPS**, or **US Mail tracking**) and fetch the latest status.

  3. Review the Status

    The resulting status (e.g., "In Transit," "Out for Delivery," "Delivered") will be displayed. If you have a specific **USPS tracking package**, the steps will match the carrier's official system.

3. Common Tracking Status Explanations

Understanding the carrier terminology is key to knowing when your **track package** will arrive:

  • **Label Created:** The carrier has been notified, but they haven't picked up the item yet.
  • **In Transit:** The package is moving through the carrier network (**United Parcel Service tracking**).
  • **Out for Delivery:** The package has left the local depot and will arrive today.
  • **Delivery Attempted:** The carrier tried to deliver but couldn't (e.g., no one was available).
  • **Delivered:** The shipment has successfully reached its destination.

**Important:** For issues like a lost package or a wrong address, please contact the specific carrier (**FedEx**, **DHL**, etc.) directly, as we only provide tracking information.
